Budget

A few things to consider about the money that goes into planning these games:

  • You should be prepared to spend about 25% more than you budget (maybe not, but you should have it as an emergency fund).
  • VERY FEW games make money off of the game (at least, player fees). If you think that this will turn a profit for you (or this is your motivator) - don't do it.

Revenue

Player Fees: Typically depends on how much you are giving players, and if there is any sort of prize associated with the game. On average, games typically charge $50-100 per day spent in the game, per player.

Photo: The Mole Ohio

Fundraising: It is not uncommon that different groups will do fundraisers (i.e., mini 1-day games, fundraising for advantages, GoFundMe, etc.) to help offset game costs.

Sponsors: For longer games, you can seek sponsorship from local restaurants, etc. in helping provide meals. Games can recognize sponsors in their edited episodes and social media.

Expenses

Cash Prize: Are you providing any sort of prize to the winner(s) of your game?

  • Will you provide any other sort of "prizes" in the game?
  • Reminder: A prize could also be a custom memento, rather than cash, and players generally find that is still "worth playing for".

Game Location: Do you have to pay any money to rent out the area (i.e., Park, Airbnb, etc.)?

  • Additional Space: Will you need to pay to rent out additional space for challenges, will you need to provide lodging to your volunteers, etc.?

Transportation: How will you and your group be getting around?

  • Will you get a rental car/van/etc for the game to transport players around?

Photo: The Golden Gates

Merchandise/Swag: Will you be providing any sort of t-shirts, buffs, souvenirs or keepsakes for the game?

  • Will you need to purchase advantages, props, etc.?
  • Do you have any decorations, banners, etc?

Equipment: What equipment will you need to purchase for the game?

  • Challenge equipment, props, materials?
  • Everyday staging equipment (i.e., chairs, tables, etc)
  • Are you filming? Will you need cameras and video equipment? Will you need mics?

Hospitality: Do you plan to provide snacks and/or meals to the players? Feed your production team too!

  • Grab and go meals are often cheaper and easier during filming.
  • Will you cater or cook any meals?
