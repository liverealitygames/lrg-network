About Us

What are Live Reality Games?

Live Reality Games (LRGs) are events where a group of strangers come together to challenge themselves socially, physically, and strategically, have fun, and fight to be the last player standing. The majority of LRGs are inspired by popular reality television shows, but games often add their own unique twists or challenges. Overall, these are fan-created games made by fans for fans to have adventure, build a community, and win bragging rights.

LRGs range greatly in production, length, and style. For example, games range in size, from smaller games in backyards and local parks with friends and family to larger games with a formal casting process that bring together people from far away. Games may run anywhere from one day to a full week. There are college clubs that run games that last an entire semester. Certain games even have a giving component, with players fundraising and competing for money to be donated to chosen non-profits or causes. Finally, while some games are unrecorded, other games are streamed live on various social media platforms for friends and family to watch or even uploaded as edited episodes on YouTube.

Some of the most well-known LRGs draw inspiration from hit reality TV shows. For example, games based on Survivor have contestants outwit, outplay, and outlast each other in a battle for survival, all while being outdoors and exposed to the elements. Games based on Big Brother similarly have players navigate social dynamics and form alliances, but inside of a house. Other games take inspiration from The Mole or The Traitors, where players compete in challenges and must discern social dynamics to determine who can or cannot be trusted. Finally, there are LRGs that take the best elements of these well-known shows to create unique and original formats.

Why Should You Play an LRG?

LRGs offer a thrilling, one-of-a-kind experience that can provide opportunities for you to challenge yourself, find community, and experience personal growth. Whether you are a fan of reality TV or simply seeking a unique adventure, here are a few reasons you should consider applying for an LRG:

  • Excitement and Challenge
    LRGs are designed to push you to your limits. In these games, you may find yourself competing in physical endurance challenges for yourself or for a team. You may need to form alliances, calculate moves, and take risks to outwit competitors. You will navigate complex and fluid social dynamics, and have to make difficult decisions under pressure. These are a chance to test your own abilities and discover strength you never knew you had!
  • Build a New Community
    LRGs bring together diverse people from all walks of life, giving you the opportunity to meet and work with new friends while forging deep connections. Whether you’re collaborating with allies or engaging in intense rivalries, you’ll have the chance to interact with others in ways that go beyond everyday life, creating unforgettable memories. As a result, these games build communities and friendships that extend beyond the game itself. You’ll be part of a dynamic group that shares similar passions, tied together through a strong and unique shared experience.
  • Personal Growth and Development
    The immersive nature of LRGs allows for profound personal development. You’ll build problem-solving skills, learn how to manage stress, improve communication, and refine your decision-making abilities. Overcoming obstacles and navigating challenges will leave you with a sense of accomplishment and newfound confidence. Furthermore, playing these games will give you a unique experience unlike any other. Whether you come out of the game victorious or not, the story of your journey—your strategy, your challenges, your victories, and even your losses—will be something you’ll cherish and share for years to come.

Playing an LRG is an opportunity to step into an immersive world of strategy, social dynamics, and unforgettable experiences. It’s your chance to challenge yourself, meet new people, and create lasting memories. Ready to play?
